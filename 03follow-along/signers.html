Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

A total of 56 delegates signed the Declaration of Independence, representing the 13 American colonies. While all are considered Founding Fathers, some of the most notable figures include

Thomas Jefferson
The primary author of the document and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, famous for his large, iconic signature.
Benjamin Franklin
The oldest signer and a renowned scientist, diplomat, and polymath.
John Adams
A key advocate for independence and the 2nd President of the United States.
Samuel Adams
A leading revolutionary organizer and leader of the Sons of Liberty.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four great state papers of the United States.
Benjamin Rush
A prominent physician and educator known as the "Father of American Psychiatry."
Edward Rutledge
The youngest signer of the Declaration at the age of 26.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton
The only Catholic signer and the longest-lived member of the group.
