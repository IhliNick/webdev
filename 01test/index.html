Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology to engage in society. It involves a set of norms and rules that guide how individuals interact within the digital world, encompassing everything from online etiquette to data privacy.

What is Digital Law?

In digital citizenship, digital law refers to the electronic responsibility for actions and deeds. It involves the creation of rules and policies that govern behavior in the online world to protect users from harm.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a formal set of rules and guidelines that define how an organization’s network, devices, and digital resources may be used.

What is Plagiarism?

plagiarism is the act of using someone else's digital work—including text, images, videos, or ideas—and presenting it as your own without proper attribution.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty (also called academic misconduct) is any type of cheating or deceptive behavior that results in a student gaining an unfair advantage or receiving credit for work that is not their own.

What are the rules for AI generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content depend on whether you are using it for school, professional work, or creative projects.
